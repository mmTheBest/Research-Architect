# Citation Support Bank

The citation support bank prevents unsupported literature statements and citation dumping.

## Distinction from exemplar learning

Exemplar papers teach structure. Citation-support papers support specific claims in the draft. A paper can serve both roles, but the role must be explicit.

## Candidate table

| Citation ID | Full Reference or Stable Identifier | Year | Source Type | Supports Section | Support Claim Sentence | Why It Fits | Verification Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Verification status

Use:

- `verified`: bibliographic details and claim support checked;
- `partial`: metadata known but claim support needs checking;
- `needs_user_file`: source likely relevant but inaccessible;
- `reject`: should not be cited.

## Use rules

- Never invent citations.
- Never cite a paper for a claim it does not support.
- Avoid filling the draft with all candidate citations.
- Prefer a coherent subset that supports the research spine.
- Mark uncertainty explicitly.
